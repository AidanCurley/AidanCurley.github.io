Reflecting on UML class diagrams

TASK: Create an object model to represent a supermarket, for example, you might like to consider the some but exclusively all of the following:

Staff, Products, Customers, Online orders, Loyalty schemes

Consider how you might represent inheritance within your model and where you might use composition.

This was only my second ever attempt at a UML class diagram, and by far the most invloved. I found it really challenging to come up with all of the necessary attributes and methods for each of the classes. I am sure that if I had to implement this, my finished product would be very different to the diagram as I discovered different things I needed a class to do, or different attributes I needed it to have.

Difficulties

The main diffficulty I uncovered was trying to deal with people who are both staff members and customers. I think this highlights some shortcomings in my current approach. I initially wanted Person to be an interface that was implemented by both the Staff and Customer classes. This means that a "real life person" would be instantiated from either the Customer or the Staff class, and so, if a staff member wanted to buy soemthing, there would be two different objects for the same person.

I remember there being an example of how to get around this issue in our reading for this week. I will have to go back and take another look at it, and will produce a second iteration of this diagram after some feedback and some more consideration.
